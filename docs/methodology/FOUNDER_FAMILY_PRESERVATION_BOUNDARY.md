# Founder-Family Preservation Boundary

## Version

`0.1.3-gllm`

## Purpose

This document preserves the boundary that StegVerse development must not normalize unbounded founder depletion as an ordinary operating condition.

Past sacrifice may establish provenance, seriousness, and origin-cost.

It must not become permission for indefinite further depletion.

## Doctrine

```text
A life-preserving system must not depend on unbounded founder depletion as a stable operating condition.
```

## Clarification

Sacrifice can exist under extreme external-threat conditions as a conscious human choice.

But sacrifice must not be hidden inside the project’s normal operating model.

It must not become:

```text
a governance model
a business model
a proof of commitment
an assumed renewable resource
```

## Family Boundary

No StegVerse development path is stable if it requires sustained degradation of the founder’s children’s quality of life, destabilizes the household, or depends on unpaid founder overextension as the normal condition of progress.

The project must move toward:

```text
income
bounded work hours
fair compensation
automation that reduces founder burden
scoped AI participation
recoverable continuity
```

## Operating Rule

Near-term StegVerse work must produce at least one of:

```text
revenue
a revenue-path artifact
automation that reduces founder workload
preservation of a breakthrough that prevents loss of continuity
```

## Provenance Rule

```text
Founder sacrifice has already occurred.

The system must now treat that sacrifice as evidence of commitment and origin-cost,
not as permission to demand indefinite further depletion.
```
